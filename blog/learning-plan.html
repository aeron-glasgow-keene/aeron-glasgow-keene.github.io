Bootcamp Learning Plan

Long term goals and career pathway:

To be a musician, digital nomad and activist, based in Europe. The skills at Dev Academy will serve me in finding work to sustain me at least financially while I work towards these goals. The impact I want to have on the world is something along the lines of: working with a team of people to fight the good fight in the developing renewable energy industry, helping other people to realise their own goals, and to be wherever I am needed to help make the world a more connected and sustainable place.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

My biggest limitations: perfectionism, pesky negative thoughts/beliefs, and my tendency to lose focus and motivation on the days when I feel "off". This is something that hasn't changed much so far through foundations, but it is something I have become more aware of as I've been continually confronted with it during the Sprint exercises.

My biggest strengths: my self-awareness, attention to detail, tenacity and honesty with myself, and occasionally remembering it's a ok to be a bit of a silly sausage sometimes.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

Other than what I already mentioned in my answer to the last question: group speaking, asking for help, and saying no to social opportunities that I don't realistically have time for during bootcamp.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

Group speaking, particularly off-the-cuff. Letting go of perfectionism. Taking initiative. Generating my own motivation and focus. Knowing my limits and setting boundaries.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

That you will do your best to help where you can, and to keep an eye out for people who are struggling to keep up. To point people in the right direction rather than giving the right answers.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp?

That I will ask for help when I need it, using the Discord server to reach out to either my team or the facilitators. I will practice my best Non-Violent Communication when working with others and providing feedback, and be honest with how I'm doing and feeling.

Foundations Learning Plan

Long term goals and career pathway:

My long term goal is to become "financially free" (i.e. retire/not need to work) by the time I'm 40, so that I can focus all my time and energy on doing what I love - making music, travelling and contributing to causes I think are worthwhile. My priority therefore is to find work in an industry that pays well (i.e. tech), looks after its employees and contributes to a good cause (such as renewable energy), while I develop my financial literacy and work towards my personal goals.

Learning Challenges and Limitations while at Dev Academy:

Something I've noticed recently is that the particular mental/emotional barriers I come up against sometimes have a message. They vary slightly each time, but it tends to be some variant of "I can't do this" or "it can't be done" or "I don't wanna" or "that isn't fair". I intend to make them the subject of reflection and therapeutic exploration as I progress through this course, as some will be coming from deeply routed beliefs from childhood that I want to challenge and change.

I have also been experiencing "brain fog" that intensifies my susceptibility to being distracted. This is something I will continue monitoring and if it becomes a serious issue I will seek professional help with. It's also fairly likely I have some kind of undiagnosed ADHD that affects my ability to focus, however I suspect my options to address this are limited.

Some things that help me to overcome these challenges and limitations include: daily sunlight and exercise in the morning, a healthy-enough diet, meditation, and staying connected with friends and family. Even when all else fails, necessity due to external pressure (e.g. a deadline) tends to invoke in me enough sheer force of will to get the job done.

Learning Strengths:

Being courageously and compassionately honest with myself; my ability to remember something (convert to muscle-memory) after I've done it once or twice; celebrating my wins - I'm fairly good at remembering to do that (although it does take conscious effort sometimes); and analytical problem-solving skills and my attention to detail.

What "Human Skills" I would like to see developed while at Dev Academy:

  • My ability to focus (and stay focused) on one thing at a time.
  • Developing methodologies for mental clarity.
  • Actively converting words to images or analogy in my head.
  • Improving my confidence with speaking in group environments.

A commitment to how I will manage my workload in this programme:

I will take one day at a time, maintaining the following routine: wake up by 8am, exercise, start working by 10am, stick to the Time Boxes and use Pomodoro's as much as feasible, have 1-2 breaks for lunch and afternoon tea, and finish the day's work by 6pm.

When working with team members, facilitators and industry/community representatives, I will act according to the Dev Academy principles (Integrity, Kindness and Effort), maintaining a clear line of communication on group projects using the most appropriate channels, and giving feedback using the ASK method (Actionable, Specific and Kind).

What I will do when things are building up and starting to get to me:

I will take a break (go for a walk, do some yoga and/or focus on something relaxing for a while) if it is reasonable/feasible. Otherwise, I will ask for help from one of my facilitators and/or team members.

A commitment as to how and where I will seek help in a timely way:

In Foundations, if I am stuck on something for more than the length of 12x Pomodoro's (i.e. 1 hour), I will ask for help using the most appropriate channel on Discord (or in person, if possible). In Bootcamp, I will follow the same rule, except asking for help after 1x Pomodoro.

What I expect from the Facilitation team:

I expect the facilitators to uphold the Dev Academy Principles (Integrity, Kindness and Effort), and to generally point me in the right direction rather than giving me the answers.

Non-EDA Schedule Commitments:

Every second Wednesday evening (from 6pm till 9pm) I will be attending a Men's Group circle, and won't be contactable by phone, email, Discord, Slack, carrier pigeon, smoke signal, or screaming my name into the ether.
