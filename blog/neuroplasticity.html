Neuroplasticity

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people.

Neuroplasticity is the idea that the brain's capacity for learning is not "fixed" from any age, contrary to popular belief. The brain is in fact "plastic" at all stages of life, meaning that it is always possible to form new neural pathways, and thereby learn new skills, as well as override even deeply-ingrained habits. Studies have shown that the perception someone has about their own ability to change has a direct impact on the person's actual ability to - a group of students that have been taught about the brain's plasticity will typically perform better than a group who have not been taught this (and therefore likely believe that the brain is "fixed").

Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your benefit.

Outside of Dev Acadmeny, I have learned a bit about self-hypnosis which is a great example of harnessing the brain's ability to rewire itself, and it is a valuable tool for rewriting limiting beliefs. This could be helpful in bootcamp when I might come across a block and need to get beyond messages of "I can't do this" or "it can't be done" etc. Repetition - going briefly over the course content learned so far at the end of each sprint/week.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

  1. Sustained focused intention - telling your brain that this is something important.
  2. Exercise/movement. Yoga - being almost off balance puts your brain into a heightened learning state
  3. Newness - novel experience
  4. Fasting
  5. Sleep! - this is when most of the remodelling-itself occurs
  6. Meditation - self-directed neuroplasticity

Growth Mindset

"Growth mindset" is the opposite of "fixed mindset", and is about believing in and working with the brain's ability to rewire itself (its neuroplasticity). It can be a powerful tool for learning new skills and concepts, and can be used to overcome limiting beliefs, such as the idea that the older you get, the harder it is to learn new things.

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

No, but it was helpful to be reminded of these ideas and how important they are for learning.

How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

I will challenge myself to go into every Sprint with a "growth mindset", i.e. an attitude of patience and willingness to not need to know everything all at once or get it perfect, and with the idea that mastery is possible for anyone with enough time and practice.
